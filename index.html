The role
As Student Coordinator for Vaze Sanskruti and part of the Dimensions committee, I planned and ran Annual Day, cultural events and college fests across multiple semesters. This is where the instinct for events was built: not in a classroom, but in the chaos of making things happen with limited resources and full accountability.

Brief to execution: venue, schedule, cross-team coordination, rehearsals, day-of logistics and contingency calls. Managing performers, volunteers and faculty simultaneously. Why it is relevant now
Every activation is structurally the same problem: a brief, a space, an audience, and the gap between the plan and reality. I have lived in that gap. I know how to close it.

Campaign ideation
Worked in team on campaign ideation for Pringles, Mother's Recipe, and BKT Tyres.
The Mother's Recipe shoot day
Attended the pre-production meeting (PPM) for the Mother's Recipe TVC before the shoot. The PPM involved frame-by-frame shot planning, costume detailing based on cultural relevance, colour palette and lighting decisions, location scouting rationale and actor selection. Every decision traced back to brand intention.

On shoot day: watched the transformation of a space into a full ad set. Lighting changed the entire mood. The director cued exact expressions. Hours of setup for seconds of footage. Went inside the vanity van. Learned that a 30-second ad demands 30+ hours of granular planning and that storytelling is not just written: it is designed, styled and choreographed.
"Changed the way I look at ads. Every choice, from the shape of a mango piece to the colour of a saree, reflects brand intention."
What I learnt
Competitor analysis is a strategic tool, not an audit exercise. And production is where brand strategy gets tested against real light, real time, real decisions. I want to be closer to that intersection.

Copywriting
82.5 Communications, Ogilvy · 2025
Writing Copy for Franklin Templeton and Mother's Recipe
Social copyOutdoor copyFinancial servicesFMCG
+ expand
What I did
Wrote candidate copy lines for Franklin Templeton social campaigns, requiring a tone that felt intelligent without being inaccessible. Lines were shortlisted by the senior copywriting team.

Also worked on hoarding copy for Mother's Recipe, a completely different discipline. Outdoor gives you two seconds and no context every word has to carry its weight.
What I learnt
Three things copywriting taught me
Medium shapes the message. Social copy and outdoor copy begin from the same consumer insight but demand completely different thinking. The format is not neutral.
Good copy is compressed research. A line that works is not clever writing it is an insight with all the excess removed. You cannot write it without first understanding the person.
The lines that don't make the cut teach you more. Understanding why a line was rejected sharpened my instinct for what actually lands.
Shortlisting decisions were made by the senior team. Specific campaign details are confidential.

What I did
Conducted in-store audits across 14+ retail locations for Naturell, a health and wellness brand studying SKU placement, shelf positioning and signage relative to competitors. Followed this with consumer feedback interviews to understand the gap between what the brand intended and what shoppers actually perceived at the point of purchase.
